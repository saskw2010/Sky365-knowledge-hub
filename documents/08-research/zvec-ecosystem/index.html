The governing strategy

One canonical hub

Sky365-knowledge-hub owns findings, decisions, gap matrices, source manifests, roadmaps, and visual documentation.

Separate forks

Important upstream repositories remain independent executable research specimens with preserved history and licenses.

No blind copying

We link, pin, inspect, test, and promote verified conclusions. We do not import every upstream file into the hub.

Fork decision rule

Fork

Strategic code, long-term tracking, experiments, patches, or potential reuse.

Link and document

Articles, tutorials, architectural references, and sources that do not need executable inspection.

Reject

Duplicated projects, untested demos, abandoned sources, or attractive tools with no role in the target architecture.

A fork is not a trophy. Every fork must produce an architecture anatomy, capability inventory, risk review, gap matrix, reuse decision, C# opportunity assessment, experiment evidence, and an upstream synchronization policy.

Public/private boundary

Public

Generic architecture, public-source analysis, reusable patterns, benchmark methods, and non-sensitive examples.

Private

Customer schemas, tenant vocabularies, production endpoints, authorization maps, unreleased commercial rules, and implementation secrets.

Linked evidence

Private code evidence remains in implementation repositories and is referenced without copying sensitive content.

Critical control: a public research hub must never become a data lake for internal repositories. It is a governed knowledge index and decision record.

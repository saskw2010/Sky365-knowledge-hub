# Sector D — B2B Sales, Growth, Partnerships, and Market Entry

**Status:** Canonical operating specification  
**Owner:** Mostafa Elnagar / SKY365  
**Purpose:** Convert market intelligence into sellable offers, qualified opportunities, partnerships, pilots, and revenue.

## Central question

> What can SKY365 sell now, to whom, through which channel, with which integration, and what is the next concrete revenue action?

## D1 — Ideal customer profiles

Track and refine ICPs for:

- Healthcare, clinics, pharmacy, diagnostics.
- Real estate and property management.
- Fleet, car rental, logistics, and field operations.
- Legal and professional services.
- Retail, commerce, and marketplace operators.
- SMEs needing ERP, CRM, payroll, attendance, expenses, and automation.
- Government and semi-government digital transformation.
- System integrators, resellers, and white-label partners.

For each ICP record: pain, buyer, budget owner, urgency, buying trigger, current workaround, competitors, required proof, and shortest path to pilot.

## D2 — Sellable offer factory

Generate concrete offers rather than generic product ideas:

- Problem statement.
- Target buyer.
- Offer name.
- Outcome promised.
- Included modules and integrations.
- Delivery scope and exclusions.
- Demo scenario.
- Pilot duration.
- Implementation effort.
- Pricing hypothesis.
- Evidence needed to sell.
- Upsell path.

Prioritize offers that can be demonstrated within 7–14 days and sold without rebuilding the platform.

## D3 — Integration-led sales

Monitor and design revenue opportunities around:

- WhatsApp, Telegram, email, calendar, voice callback, SMS.
- Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, Slack, GitHub.
- Odoo, Oracle, NetSuite, Shopify, WooCommerce, Magento, nopCommerce.
- Payment gateways, accounting, payroll, HR, CRM, e-signature, identity, maps, logistics, and government APIs.
- MCP, API gateways, ETL, events, connectors, and data migration.

For each integration ask:

- Which customer pain does it remove?
- Is it a product feature, paid connector, implementation service, or strategic partnership?
- Can it create recurring revenue?
- Does it shorten procurement or create lock-in?

## D4 — B2B pipeline and account intelligence

Track:

- Named prospects.
- Decision makers and influencers.
- Lead source.
- Sector and use case.
- Stage: discovered, qualified, discovery, demo, pilot, proposal, negotiation, won, lost, nurture.
- Expected value and probability.
- Required next action and owner.
- Objections and missing proof.
- Follow-up date.

No lead may remain as “interesting company” without a next action or explicit rejection reason.

## D5 — Partnerships and channels

Evaluate:

- Resellers.
- System integrators.
- Incubators and accelerators.
- Universities and research centers.
- Telecom and cloud providers.
- Accounting, legal, and consulting partners.
- Government innovation programs.
- Referral and white-label models.

Classify each relationship as sales channel, implementation partner, technology partner, investor access, credibility signal, or market-entry support.

## D6 — Geography and market-entry plays

Primary markets:

- Qatar.
- Abu Dhabi and UAE.
- Kuwait.
- Saudi Arabia.
- Oman.
- Bahrain.
- Spain, Italy, Finland, Bosnia, and Canada when materially relevant.

For Qatar and Abu Dhabi, study:

- Enterprise and government buyers.
- Innovation programs and challenge statements.
- Procurement routes.
- Local partner requirements.
- Pilot opportunities.
- Sector-specific budgets.
- Integration demand.
- Founder residency only as an enabling factor, not the primary business objective.

## D7 — Pricing and packaging

Develop:

- Subscription tiers.
- Implementation fees.
- Paid connectors.
- Usage-based AI pricing.
- Industry packs.
- White-label and reseller pricing.
- Pilot pricing.
- Enterprise support and SLA.

Every price must link to customer value, cost to serve, sales friction, and expansion revenue.

## D8 — Sales experiments

Run small measurable experiments:

- One-page offer.
- Targeted outbound sequence.
- Live demo.
- Paid discovery workshop.
- Pilot proposal.
- Partner pitch.
- Industry webinar.
- Case-study recreation.

For each experiment define hypothesis, audience, message, channel, sample size, success metric, cost, result, and decision.

## D9 — Competitive and procurement intelligence

Track only developments that affect selling:

- Competitor pricing and packaging.
- New integrations.
- Customer wins.
- Procurement frameworks.
- Public tenders.
- Vendor shutdowns.
- Security or compliance gaps.
- Buyer preference for on-premise, private cloud, local hosting, or sovereign AI.

## D10 — Daily sales output

The daily report must produce:

1. One highest-priority revenue opportunity.
2. One concrete offer or offer refinement.
3. One named target account or partner class.
4. One integration that improves sellability.
5. One next commercial action with owner and deadline.
6. One item to reject or stop researching.

## Quality gate

Reject generic “build an AI platform” ideas. A valid item must identify buyer, pain, offer, integration, proof, commercial path, and next action.

## 4. Story S02 — Sector Architecture

### What was discussed

New requests must not generate new automations. They should be routed to an existing sector and added as items or segments.

### Decision

A sector is created only when a subject has a genuinely distinct decision owner, evidence system, and recurring output that cannot be represented inside an existing sector.

### Critical correction

B2B and Fundraising are separate business functions:

- B2B owns customers, offers, pilots, integrations, channels, and revenue.
- Fundraising owns investors, grants, capital readiness, and capital allocation.

### Restart point

Review each sector through a survey: current items, missing items, duplicates, source map, decision outputs, and user additions.

## 9. Story S07 — Skyverse and Swarm AI

### What was discussed

Skyverse is intended to remain the latest and most complete internal SKY365 edition. It includes integration, implementation planning, output schemas, advanced agents, and future swarm capabilities.

### Desired swarm experience

- Agents communicate in a shared channel.
- Agents debate and critique.
- One may act as leader or supervisor.
- Agents delegate and verify work.
- The user can talk to individual agents and the swarm.
- Context graphs are generated and updated.
- Agents can be paused, replaced, or reassigned.

### Decision

Swarm AI is a serious strategic roadmap, but it must be phased rather than built as a complete autonomous society immediately.

### Current roadmap direction

**NOW**

- Canonical Skyverse Schema V0.
- Identity and tenant resolution.
- Roles and policy resolution.
- Remote MCP/App Gateway V0.
- Tool registry.
- Output schema.
- Audit trace.
- One external connector prototype.
- One B2B-linked demonstration.

**NEXT**

- Shared swarm channel.
- Supervisor/leader agent.
- Delegation.
- Debate and critique.
- Verification agent.
- Context graph.
- Coordinated memory.
- Recovery and rollback.

**LATER**

- Dynamic topology.
- Advanced leader election.
- Agent replacement and sacrifice policies.
- Self-evolving swarms.
- Connector marketplace.

### Restart point

Define one measurable swarm use case, not generic swarm infrastructure. Candidate: multi-agent proposal review, contract review, or ERP exception resolution.

## 10. Story S08 — Connectors, MCP, Authentication, and Roles

### What was discussed

The user asked how SKY365/Skyverse can be connected to ChatGPT, Claude, and other platforms so those clients can read and act on the system.

### Decision

Prefer one standards-based remote MCP/App Gateway rather than separate connector cores per model.

Architecture direction:

`External AI Client → Platform Adapter → Remote MCP/App Gateway → Skyverse Identity/Policy Resolution → Tools, Resources, Workflows, Agents`

### Identity rule

External platforms do not decide internal roles. Skyverse resolves:

- External subject.
- Internal user.
- Tenant membership.
- Roles.
- Policies.
- Entitlements.
- Allowed tools and resources.

### Open work

- OAuth/OIDC flow.
- External account linking schema.
- Tenant resolution.
- Consent and approval scopes.
- Read versus write tools.
- Audit and revocation.
- ChatGPT adapter.
- Claude MCP adapter.

### Restart point

Create ADR: `Skyverse Remote MCP/App Gateway and Internal Authorization Boundary`.

## 13. Story S11 — Idea Pool and Business Plan Changer

### What was discussed

The user rejected rigid adherence to a fixed path. The world changes, and some ideas legitimately change the business plan.

### Decision

Maintain one Idea Pool with three classes:

1. General Culture.
2. Strategic.
3. Business Plan Changer.

### Changer control

A potential changer must state:

- What changed.
- Evidence.
- Which assumption is invalidated.
- Upside and downside.
- Switching cost.
- Time sensitivity.
- What must stop.
- What must start.
- Decision and review trigger.

### Balance rule

Avoid both extremes:

- Defending an obsolete plan.
- Abandoning execution for every attractive idea.

### Restart point

Create a persistent Idea Pool file/schema and migrate current ideas into the three classifications.

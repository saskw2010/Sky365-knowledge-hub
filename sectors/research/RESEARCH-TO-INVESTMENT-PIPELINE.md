# Research-to-Investment Pipeline

## Principle

Investment presentation follows research validation. The Investment Sector does not invent maturity; it packages verified opportunity.

```text
Discovery
→ Research track
→ Evidence collection
→ Gap analysis
→ Experiment
→ Decision
→ Product concept
→ Pilot definition
→ Investment readiness review
→ Investment opportunity
→ Investor landing page
→ Data room
```

## Promotion gates

| Gate | Required evidence |
|---|---|
| G0 — Discovered | Problem or opportunity recorded |
| G1 — Researched | Internal and external evidence reviewed |
| G2 — Technically plausible | Architecture and constraints documented |
| G3 — Experimented | Benchmark, spike, prototype, or pilot evidence |
| G4 — Product candidate | Customer, value proposition, and product boundary defined |
| G5 — Investment candidate | Milestones, use of funds, risks, and investment structure defined |
| G6 — Promoted | Approved for investor portfolio and landing page |

## Investment readiness score

Each candidate is scored from 0 to 5 on:

- Problem clarity
- Customer urgency
- Internal evidence
- Technical feasibility
- Differentiation
- Reusability across Sky365
- Revenue model
- Go-to-market access
- Regulatory and security readiness
- Team and execution readiness

A project should normally reach at least 35/50 before public promotion.

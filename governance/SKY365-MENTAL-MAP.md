# Sky365 Evolving Mental Map

## Navigation principle

The map is allowed to evolve. The requirement is not that the first structure be perfect; the requirement is that every knowledge item has:

1. a parent;
2. a domain;
3. a source;
4. a status;
5. a route;
6. a next action.

## Level 0 — Purpose and ownership

- Why Sky365 exists.
- What WytSky must own.
- What can remain replaceable infrastructure.
- Product, data, evaluation, safety, and institutional-memory ownership.

## Level 1 — WytSky / Sky365 universe

- Business and investment.
- Product platforms.
- Knowledge and research.
- Academy and learning.
- Delivery and implementation.
- Operations, governance, and evidence.

## Level 2 — Platform pillars

### P01 — Agentic Platform
Runtime, planning, tools, MCP, memory, routing, verification, recovery, and observability.

### P02 — Knowledge and Context
RAG, knowledge graphs, semantic vocabulary, context engineering, sources, provenance, and institutional memory.

### P03 — Safety and Governance
Policy, DraftOnly, approvals, isolation, secrets, prompt-injection defense, audit, rollback, and tenant boundaries.

### P04 — Multimodal Intelligence
Documents, OCR, image, voice, video, avatars, media pipelines, and multimodal ingestion.

### P05 — Skills and Behavior
Prompt intelligence, skills, workflows, selective fine-tuning, evaluation packs, and behavior regression.

### P06 — Experience and Interaction
Portal, academy, stories, design studio, dashboards, chat, voice, visual maps, and product interfaces.

### P07 — Domain Platforms
ERP, school, healthcare/pharmacy, legal, real estate, fleet/rental, commerce, and other industry packs.

### P08 — Research and Investment
Research radar, experiments, adopt/watch/reject decisions, project portfolio, investor portfolio, and research-to-product pipeline.

## Level 4 — Knowledge artifacts

Every capability may contain:

- Blueprint (`BP`).
- Architecture Decision Record (`ADR`).
- Research Track (`RT`).
- Roadmap (`RM`).
- Implementation Evidence (`IE`).
- Evaluation Pack (`EV`).
- Lesson Learned (`LL`).
- Visual Experience (`VX`).
- Source Snapshot (`SS`).

## Session-to-knowledge path

`Discussion → Classification → Source/Evidence → Blueprint or Research → Decision → Roadmap → Implementation → Validation → Lesson → Navigation update`

## Rearrangement triggers

Rearrange the map when:

- one domain contains unrelated material;
- the same capability appears under several parents;
- a source repository starts acting like a canonical repository;
- pages have no inbound route;
- visual applications and source snapshots are mixed;
- implementation evidence is confused with future architecture;
- business, research, and product decisions are not connected;
- language separation becomes necessary.

## Current honest state

- The upper hierarchy is usable.
- Canonical document domains exist.
- Visual entry pages exist.
- Source snapshots and exact asset migration remain incomplete.
- Repository census is now explicit but must be expanded repository by repository.
- Rearrangement is an active operating method, not a one-time cleanup.
